Paragraphs

Times New Roman was commissioned in 1931 by the British Newspaper, The Times. It is a serif typeface and due to its simple and solid design, has become one of the most popular fonts. Despite its name, it has no tie to ancient Rome, and is merely named after the roman or Antiqua font family.
